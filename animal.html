Indigenous Animals

Indigenous animals are species that are native to a particular geographic region and have evolved and adapted to that specific environment over a long period. These animals play crucial roles in their ecosystems and are often well-suited to the local climate, vegetation, and other ecological factors of their native habitats. Indigenous animals are an integral part of the natural biodiversity of a region.

Examples of indigenous animals can include mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, and invertebrates. These species have naturally occurred in a specific area without human intervention. They are adapted to the local conditions and contribute to the overall ecological balance of their ecosystems.

Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus inornatus)

The sloth bear is a species of bear native to the Indian subcontinent, including Sri Lanka. These bears have a distinct appearance with long shaggy fur and a white V-shaped mark on their chest..

Toque Macaque (Macaca sinica)

The toque macaque is another primate species found in Sri Lanka. These monkeys have a toque-like crown of hair on their heads and inhabit a variety of habitats, including forests and urban areas.
